The Problem

Yangon faces major water challenges due to rapid urbanization, population growth, and aging infrastructure. The city relies on reservoirs and lakes, but supply is often inconsistent and affected by seasonal flooding and dry periods.

How People Access Water

Piped reservoir water

Most homes, apartments, and businesses in downtown Yangon receive treated water from reservoirs such as Gyobyu and Phugyi. The water is cleaned at treatment plants and distributed through city pipelines.

Groundwater Wells

Some buildings and businesses use underground wells to pump groundwater, especially when city water pressure is low or supply is interrupted.

Stored and delivered water

During shortages, people often rely on rooftop storage tanks or water delivered by private water trucks to ensure a steady supply for daily use.

Why Water Matters

Water is one of the world’s most valuable resources. It supports human life, agriculture, ecosystems, and sustainable cities.

As urban populations grow and climate conditions continue to change, many communities face water shortages, pollution, and inefficient distribution systems.

Through innovation, smart monitoring, and community awareness, we aim to promote cleaner and more sustainable water management for future generations.

Sustainable water options

Rainwater harvesting systems

Buildings can collect and store rainwater from rooftops in large tanks for cleaning, flushing, and even drinking after treatment. This reduces pressure on city pipelines and groundwater. UN-Habitat projects in Yangon have already shown that large community rainwater tanks can provide reliable year-round water supplies.


Managed groundwater use with recharge systems

Groundwater can remain reliable if extraction is controlled and combined with recharge methods like infiltration wells and permeable pavements that allow rainwater to refill underground aquifers. Studies warn that uncontrolled pumping in Yangon may cause land subsidence, so regulated use is important.


Water recycling and decentralized wastewater treatment

Treated greywater and wastewater can be reused for toilets, landscaping, and cooling systems in apartments and commercial buildings. Decentralized treatment systems (DEWATS) are considered more sustainable and resilient for Yangon because they reduce waste and lower demand for fresh water.
